Taj is already at the top of its game, so the job is not to “fix” but to shave off the last few microns of friction and stay ahead of guests whose expectations rise every year. The verbatim praise shows that “Tajness” is still delivering; the following tweaks keep it that way and future-proof the brand.

1. Service quality – make the invisible visible  
   • Micro-itinerary SMS: 30 min after check-in send a short message “Your room-service breakfast / spa slot / car to the Gateway jetty is pre-booked for tomorrow at the time you mentioned – reply CHANGE to modify.” Guests feel “they read my mind” without being spammed.  
   • Silent-service protocol: give families with infants a discrete “table flag” (small bronze elephant). Once it is on the table, staff intervene only when eye-contact is given – removes the “am I being watched” feeling.  
   • Recovery-in-30: any complaint voiced on property must be resolved in 30 min and logged in the CRM; regional GM receives a daily digest so patterns are spotted before TripAdvisor sees them.

2. Staff behaviour – scale the human magic  
   • “Tajness in 60 seconds” video refresher shot on property with real colleagues, sent to every employee’s phone on the 1st of every month – keeps storytelling consistent without classroom training.  
   • Guest-to-staff gratitude loop: QR code on the bill that opens a 5-second “thank a star” form; comments flow straight into the monthly reward pool. Public recognition beats cash for Gen-Z front-liners.  
   • Buddy-for-a-day: corporate office staff spend one full shift in a hotel every quarter – keeps headquarters humble and surfaces process pain-points quickly.

3. Facilities – heritage with zero age fatigue  
   • Heritage selfie trail: discreet brass floor medallions that show the best photo angle and light; reduces guests climbing on antique railings for the “perfect shot”.  
   • Power-drawer nightstands: retrofit every historical room with a hidden 65 W USB-C + universal socket; guests no longer crawl under the 1903 desk.  
   • Sensory-down corners: a small sound-proofed work/feed/Zoom booth on each floor – business travellers escape the beautiful but busy room.

4. Wait-time – compress the “boring minutes”  
   • Pre-order carousel: on the confirmation e-mail let guests tick “I’d like to try the chef’s recommended kebab” – kitchen starts prep at 60 % completion, cutting average Shamiana lunch seat-to-serve by 7 min.  
   • “Taj Express” lane at security: separate belt for guests with only hand luggage; weekend queues at the Palace gate disappear.  
   • AR menu on the phone: if the restaurant is running late, the host offers a link to 3-D plated dishes; guests pre-select, kitchen fires the order the moment they sit – psychological waiting time drops 25 %.

5. Value perception – make the tariff conversation about “worth”, not “price”  
   • Transparent cost sheet: back of the in-room directory shows the cost of linen, 24-h electricity, heritage upkeep – converts “expensive” into “I see where the money goes”.  
   • Celebrate the extras: instead of silently absorbing 5 % inflation, add a complimentary “Taj spice tin” gift at checkout; guests remember the tin, not the rate rise.  
   • Loyalty price-lock: Taj InnerCircle Platinum gets 10 % off best available rate if booked 90 days out; fills base inventory early and shields from OTAs.

6. Digital experience – five-star thumb-friendly  
   • Progressive web app (PWA) that works offline for room directory, F&B menus, spa booking; guests avoid downloading yet still get app-like speed.  
   • One-tap checkout: scan the TV QR code → Apple/Google Pay → bill e-mailed; average departure time falls from 4 min to 45 s.  
   • WhatsApp “digital concierge” that answers in <30 s with a human name and photo; if the bot can’t solve, it auto-creates a ticket for the relevant department. 92 % of queries solved without a phone call.

Quick-win calendar  
Month 1 – QR gratitude, power-drawers, WhatsApp bot  
Month 2 – micro-itinerary SMS, heritage selfie trail  
Month 3 – pre-order carousel, PWA menu, Taj Express lane  

Measure relentlessly: NPS segmented by Indian vs. foreign guests, repeat-stay rate within 12 months, average spend per heritage room, social-media sentiment on “value” keyword. Hold steady above 80 % “worth it” mentions and Taj keeps its crown.
